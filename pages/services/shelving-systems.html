Industrial shelving systems are storage structures designed for smaller items, cartons, and hand-pick inventory that don't require full pallet positions. Unlike pallet racking, shelving is accessed by hand rather than forklift and offers more levels in less vertical space. Rack Storage Solutions supplies wire shelving, steel shelving, rivet shelving, and boltless systems for warehouses, distribution centers, stockrooms, and parts departments nationwide.

Shelving Types We Supply

Wire Shelving

Open wire design allows air circulation and sprinkler penetration. Easy to clean, adjustable heights, and NSF-certified options available.

  • Excellent for food service and healthcare
  • Chrome, stainless, or epoxy-coated finishes
  • Light to medium-duty capacities
  • Quick assembly, no tools required

Steel Shelving (Closed)

Solid steel shelves provide a stable surface for boxes, bins, and irregular items. Ideal for parts storage and archive applications.

  • Clean, professional appearance
  • Heavy-duty options available
  • Adjustable shelf heights
  • Add dividers for small parts organization

Rivet Shelving (Boltless)

Heavy-duty shelving with easy boltless assembly. Particle board or steel decks available. Popular for bulk storage and stockrooms.

  • Fast assembly - no bolts or clips
  • High capacities (500-1,500 lbs/shelf)
  • Cost-effective for large installations
  • Add-on units for continuous rows

Clip Shelving

Traditional industrial shelving with clip-together assembly. Highly configurable with many accessory options.

  • Extremely versatile configurations
  • Dividers, bins, drawers available
  • Mobile aisle systems possible
  • Ideal for parts departments

How to Choose the Right Shelving

Consider These Factors:

Load Requirements

What will you store and how much does it weigh? Light-duty wire shelving handles different loads than heavy-duty rivet shelving.

Environment

Food service? Healthcare? Humid conditions? Certain finishes and materials are better suited to specific environments.

Access Frequency

High-pick areas need shelving optimized for accessibility. Archive storage can use denser configurations.

Organization Needs

Small parts may need bins and dividers. Cartons might work with open shelves. Consider what accessories you'll need.
