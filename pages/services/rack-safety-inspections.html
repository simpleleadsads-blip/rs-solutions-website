Rack safety inspections are systematic evaluations of pallet racking systems to identify damage, assess structural integrity, verify load capacities, and ensure compliance with OSHA regulations and ANSI/RMI standards. Rack Storage Solutions provides professional inspections that help warehouses maintain safe operations, avoid citations, and prevent costly accidents from rack failures.

Why Rack Inspections Matter

Damaged rack fails. When it does, the consequences can be severe.

Worker Safety

Rack collapses cause serious injuries and fatalities. Regular inspections catch damage before failure.

OSHA Compliance

OSHA requires employers to provide safe workplaces. Damaged rack can result in citations and fines.

Inventory Protection

A rack collapse destroys product and halts operations. Prevention is far cheaper than recovery.

Insurance & Liability

Documentation of regular inspections demonstrates due diligence and supports insurance claims.

The Numbers

  • Forklift impacts are the #1 cause of rack damage
  • Damaged uprights can lose 50%+ of their capacity
  • OSHA fines for rack violations can exceed $15,000 per incident
  • Rack collapse claims average hundreds of thousands in damages

What We Inspect

Uprights / Columns

Checking for bends, dents, rust, weld failures, and impact damage that reduces load capacity.

Beams

Assessing for bowing, connector damage, missing safety clips, and overloading signs.

Bracing & Connections

Verifying cross-bracing is intact, anchors are secure, and all connections are properly engaged.

Anchoring

Confirming baseplates are properly anchored and floor anchors are intact.

Plumb & Level

Measuring rack alignment to ensure it's within tolerance and hasn't shifted over time.

Load Capacities

Verifying capacity labels are present and loads don't exceed rated limits.

How Often Should You Inspect?

Annual Professional Inspection

RMI recommends annual inspections by a qualified inspector. This comprehensive evaluation catches damage that daily walkthroughs miss and creates documentation for compliance.

Regular Internal Walkthroughs

Supplement professional inspections with weekly or monthly internal checks. Train staff to spot obvious damage and report issues immediately.

Consider More Frequent Inspections If:

  • High forklift traffic
  • History of impacts
  • Heavy loads near capacity
  • Seismic activity in your area
  • New or inexperienced operators
  • Insurance or customer requirements
