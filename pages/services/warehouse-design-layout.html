Warehouse design and layout is the process of planning how racking, shelving, aisles, staging areas, and workstations are arranged within a warehouse to optimize storage capacity, workflow efficiency, and safety. Rack Storage Solutions provides professional warehouse design services including needs assessment, space planning, CAD drawings, 3D visualizations, and coordination with equipment and installation—delivering layouts that work for your specific operation.

Why Good Warehouse Design Matters

The right layout can transform your operation. The wrong one creates daily problems.

A well-designed warehouse maximizes every square foot, reduces travel time for pickers, improves safety, and adapts as your business grows. Poor design leads to wasted space, bottlenecks, damaged product, and frustrated workers.

Increase Storage Capacity

Optimized layouts often increase pallet positions by 20-40% without expanding your facility.

Improve Workflow Efficiency

Strategic placement reduces travel time, speeds order picking, and improves throughput.

Plan for Growth

Modular designs allow expansion and reconfiguration as your business changes.

What We Need From You

The more information you provide, the better your design will be.

Building Information

  • Floor plan or building dimensions
  • Clear height (floor to bottom of obstruction)
  • Column locations and sizes
  • Dock door positions
  • Any existing equipment staying in place

Operation Information

  • What products you store (size, weight, type)
  • Typical pallet dimensions and weights
  • SKU count and inventory levels
  • How product flows (receiving → storage → shipping)
  • Picking method (full pallet, case pick, each pick)
  • Forklift types and lift heights

Key Design Considerations

Clear Height Utilization

Vertical space is often underutilized. We design to take advantage of your full clear height.

Aisle Width Optimization

Narrower aisles = more racking, but equipment must fit. We balance density with operability.

Product Slotting Strategy

Fast movers near shipping, slow movers in corners. Strategic placement reduces travel time.

Traffic Flow

One-way aisles, cross-aisles, and staging areas designed to minimize congestion.

Code Compliance

Fire egress, sprinkler clearance, and seismic requirements built into every design.

Future Flexibility

Modular designs that can expand or reconfigure as your business changes.
