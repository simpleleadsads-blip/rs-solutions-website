Pallet rack permitting is required by many jurisdictions before installing racking systems above certain heights or load capacities. Permits ensure installations meet building codes, fire codes, and seismic requirements. Rack Storage Solutions provides permitting support including PE-stamped engineered drawings, seismic calculations, permit application preparation, and coordination with local building departments—taking the complexity out of compliance.

Do I Need a Permit for Pallet Racking?

Permit requirements vary by jurisdiction, but common triggers include:

  • Height thresholds: Many areas require permits for rack over 8 feet, 12 feet, or a specific height
  • Load thresholds: Some jurisdictions trigger permits based on total storage load
  • Seismic zones: California and other seismic areas have strict engineering requirements
  • Fire code: Rack layout affects sprinkler requirements and egress
  • New construction: Rack in new buildings often requires permit as part of tenant improvement

The consequences of skipping permits: Installing without required permits can result in fines, forced removal, insurance issues, and liability exposure if accidents occur.

Quick Reference

Generally requires a permit:

  • Rack over 8-12 feet (varies by jurisdiction)
  • Any rack in California
  • Rack in new construction or major renovation
  • Very high storage loads

Often permit-exempt:

  • Low-height rack (under threshold)
  • Some existing buildings with minor additions
  • Certain rural jurisdictions

The Permit Process

Here's what to expect when permits are required.

1

Requirements Research

We contact the local building department to confirm permit requirements, fees, and submittal requirements for your specific project and location.

2

Engineering & Drawing Preparation

Our engineering team creates PE-stamped drawings and calculations meeting local code requirements. Typical turnaround is 5-10 business days.

3

Permit Submission

We submit the application package to the building department. Some jurisdictions accept electronic submissions; others require in-person filing.

4

Plan Check Review

The building department reviews the submittal. Review times vary from same-day to several weeks depending on jurisdiction. We respond to any comments or correction requests.

5

Permit Issuance & Inspection

Once approved, the permit is issued and installation can proceed. Final inspection by building department verifies installation matches approved plans.
