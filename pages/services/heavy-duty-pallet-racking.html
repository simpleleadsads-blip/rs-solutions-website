What is Heavy Duty Pallet Racking?

Heavy duty pallet racking is a warehouse storage system built to safely support palletized inventory at height. Unlike light-duty shelving, heavy duty rack is engineered to handle substantial loads—typically 2,000 pounds per beam level or more—using steel uprights, horizontal beams, and industrial anchoring.

These systems maximize vertical space in warehouses, distribution centers, and manufacturing facilities. By storing pallets at multiple levels, businesses can significantly increase storage capacity without expanding their facility footprint.

Types of Pallet Racking Systems

Different applications call for different racking configurations. Here are the most common types we supply and install.

Selective Pallet Rack

The most common and versatile type. Every pallet is directly accessible from the aisle, making it ideal for operations with diverse SKUs and FIFO inventory management.

  • 100% selectivity - access any pallet instantly
  • Works with any forklift type
  • Easy to reconfigure and expand
  • Best for: high SKU count, varied inventory

Drive-In / Drive-Through Rack

High-density storage where forklifts drive directly into the rack structure. Pallets are stored on rails, allowing deep storage of like products.

  • Up to 75% more storage than selective
  • Best for LIFO inventory (or FIFO with drive-through)
  • Ideal for bulk storage of same SKU
  • Best for: cold storage, seasonal inventory

Push-Back Rack

Pallets are stored on nested carts that roll on inclined rails. Loading pushes existing pallets back; unloading lets them roll forward. High density with aisle-side loading.

  • 2-6 pallets deep per lane
  • No need to enter the rack
  • Faster throughput than drive-in
  • Best for: medium SKU count with multiple pallets each

Cantilever Rack

Open-front design with arms extending from vertical columns. No front uprights means easy loading of long, bulky, or irregularly shaped items.

  • Perfect for lumber, pipe, tubing, furniture
  • Single-sided or double-sided configurations
  • Adjustable arm heights
  • Best for: building materials, manufacturing

How Much Does Pallet Racking Cost?

New pallet racking typically costs $50-$200 per pallet position, depending on height, beam capacity, and configuration. This includes uprights, beams, and standard hardware. Wire decking, column protectors, and installation are typically additional. Used pallet rack can reduce costs by 40-60%.

Factors That Affect Price

  • Rack height (taller = heavier steel)
  • Load capacity requirements
  • Structural vs. roll-formed steel
  • Rack type (selective vs. specialty)
  • Accessories (wire deck, protectors)
  • Installation complexity
  • Permitting requirements
  • New vs. used components

Structural rack uses hot-rolled structural steel shapes (like I-beams) and is typically welded. It's extremely durable and resists forklift impact well. Roll-formed rack uses cold-rolled steel bent into shape and connected with clips or bolts. It's less expensive and easier to adjust but less impact-resistant. We recommend structural for high-traffic areas and roll-formed for most standard applications.
