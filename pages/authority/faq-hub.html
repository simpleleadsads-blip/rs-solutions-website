Pricing & Costs

New pallet racking typically costs $50-$200 per pallet position, depending on height, load capacity, and rack type. This includes uprights and beams. Additional costs include:

  • Wire decking: $15-$30 per deck
  • Column protectors: $30-$60 each
  • Installation: $30-$80 per upright
  • Permitting/engineering: $1,500-$5,000 (if required)

Used rack can save 40-60% on equipment costs. We provide detailed quotes with all costs itemized.

We don't offer direct financing, but we work with several equipment financing companies and can refer you to options. Many businesses use equipment financing to spread costs over 2-5 years. Leasing is also an option for some projects.

Our quotes itemize everything separately so you can see exactly what you're paying for: equipment (uprights, beams, decking, accessories), installation labor, freight/delivery, and permitting if applicable. No hidden fees—what you see is what you pay.

We don't have a strict minimum, but very small orders (just a few uprights) may not be cost-effective once you factor in freight. For small projects, we're happy to quote and let you decide. We can also recommend local options if that makes more sense for your situation.

Products & Equipment

We supply all major types of pallet racking:

  • Selective rack: Most common type with direct access to every pallet
  • Drive-in/drive-through: High-density storage for bulk inventory
  • Push-back rack: Dense storage with cart-based LIFO flow
  • Cantilever rack: For long, bulky items like lumber and pipe
  • Pallet flow: Gravity-fed FIFO storage for high-volume picking

Teardrop is the most common beam connection pattern in North America. The teardrop-shaped holes in the upright accept clips from various manufacturers, making it largely universal. Keystone (and other proprietary patterns) use different hole shapes and are only compatible with specific brands. We primarily stock teardrop-compatible rack for maximum flexibility.

We offer rack systems from standard warehouse capacities (3,000-5,000 lbs per beam level) up to heavy-duty industrial capacities (10,000+ lbs per level). Upright capacities and beam capacities are matched to your specific requirements. We engineer systems based on your actual loads, not generic specs.

Permitting & Engineering

It depends on your location and project. Many jurisdictions require permits for rack over a certain height (often 8-12 feet) or above load thresholds. California and other seismic zones have strict requirements. We determine requirements for your specific location and handle the permitting process if needed.

PE-stamped drawings are engineering documents signed and sealed by a licensed Professional Engineer (PE). They include layout plans, structural calculations, and specifications that demonstrate the rack system meets code requirements. Most building departments require PE-stamped drawings for permitted rack installations.

Engineering and drawing preparation takes 5-10 business days. Building department review varies widely—from same-day approval in some areas to 4-6 weeks in busy jurisdictions. We research typical timelines for your location and can expedite where possible.
