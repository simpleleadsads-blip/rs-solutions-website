Retail Distribution Challenges

High SKU Variety

Retail assortments mean thousands of products in one facility—everything from small accessories to furniture.

Case Pick Volume

Most retail replenishment happens at the case level, not full pallets. Picking speed directly impacts labor costs.

Store-Ready Shipping

Many operations require department-segregated or store-planogram-sequenced orders for efficient restocking.

Seasonal Peaks

Holiday, back-to-school, and promotional surges require capacity flexibility beyond everyday operations.

Cross-Dock Operations

Some products flow through without storage. DC layouts must support both storage and flow-through efficiently.

E-Commerce Integration

Many retail DCs now fulfill online orders alongside store replenishment—different workflows in one building.

Storage Systems for Retail Distribution

Selective Pallet Reserve

The foundation of most retail DCs. Direct access to every pallet position supports diverse SKU profiles and flexible inventory management.

  • 100% selectivity for any SKU, any time
  • Easy reconfiguration as assortments change
  • Vertical storage maximizes building cube
  • Compatible with standard lift equipment

Best for: Bulk reserve storage, high-SKU variety, receiving staging

Carton Flow Pick Modules

Gravity-fed case pick lanes where product automatically advances as cases are picked. The most efficient configuration for high-volume case picking.

  • Cases roll forward automatically (FIFO)
  • Multiple pick faces in vertical stack
  • Reduces picker walk time dramatically
  • Integrates with conveyor takeaway

Best for: High-velocity case picks, store replenishment, fast-moving SKUs

Pallet Flow for Full-Case

When full pallets feed case pick areas. Pallets flow forward on gravity rollers as front pallets are depleted.

  • FIFO rotation for date-sensitive goods
  • Automatic replenishment presentation
  • Separates replenishment from picking aisles
  • High-volume, consistent SKU support

Best for: High-velocity items, food/beverage retail, automated replenishment

Pick-to-Belt Systems

Integrated pick modules with conveyor running through the center. Pickers place cases directly on the belt for sortation.

  • Eliminates cart handling
  • Cases flow directly to sortation
  • Works with zone picking strategies
  • High throughput per labor hour

Best for: High-volume DCs, multi-store orders, automated sortation

Hybrid Storage & Pick Layouts

Retail DCs rarely use just one storage type. The most efficient facilities combine bulk reserve storage with dedicated pick modules—and we design layouts that integrate both seamlessly.

Reserve/Forward Configuration

Bulk inventory in high-density reserve areas feeds forward pick locations. Replenishment paths designed for efficiency.

Velocity-Based Zoning

Fast movers in carton flow. Medium velocity in static pick. Slow movers picked from reserve. Slotting matched to movement.

Department Clustering

Storage organized by store department or category for efficient store-ready order building.

Cross-Dock Integration

Clear staging lanes and flow paths for products that ship through without storage.

Special Retail DC Considerations

Conveyor Integration

Rack positions planned around conveyor routing. Induction points, takeaway lanes, and merge points factored into design.

Mezzanine Pick Levels

Elevated pick platforms above pallet storage multiply case pick faces in the same footprint.

Sortation Staging

Lanes and accumulation areas for store-specific order consolidation before shipping.

Returns Processing

Dedicated areas for retail returns inspection, reconditioning, and put-back to stock.

Security Caging

Wire partition areas for high-value or high-shrink merchandise requiring controlled access.

Seasonal Flex Space

Areas designed to convert between bulk storage and overflow pick locations for peak seasons.

Retail Distribution Questions

It's about right-sizing each zone. High-velocity items that get picked constantly go in low-density, high-accessibility carton flow where every second saved adds up. Slow-moving items can live in dense reserve storage and get picked directly or replenished to forward positions occasionally. The key is matching storage type to SKU velocity and pick frequency.

Carton flow is more expensive but pays back quickly for high-velocity picks. Cases present automatically at the pick face, reducing reach time and ensuring FIFO. Static shelving works for slower items where the extra investment in flow doesn't pencil out. Most retail DCs use both—flow for top movers, static for the long tail of slower-moving SKUs.

Flexibility is designed in. Selective rack with adjustable beams accommodates different product sizes. Forward pick locations can be re-slotted as assortments change. Some operations designate flex zones that convert between bulk storage and overflow picking during peak seasons. The goal is a system that adapts without major reconfiguration projects every season.
