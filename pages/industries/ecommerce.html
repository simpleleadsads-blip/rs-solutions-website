Storage Systems for E-Commerce

Each-Pick Shelving

Industrial shelving for small to medium items picked individually. The backbone of e-commerce pick modules.

  • Wire shelving: Good visibility, airflow, fire sprinkler penetration
  • Steel shelving: Heavy-duty for dense small-item storage
  • Bin/divider systems: Organized storage for small parts and accessories
  • Adjustable levels: Reconfigure as product mix changes

Best for: Small items, high SKU density, each-pick operations

Carton Flow Rack

Tilted roller or wheel lanes automatically present the next carton as picks are made. FIFO rotation built in.

  • Gravity-fed FIFO rotation
  • Reduced reaching and bending
  • Clear sightlines to pick locations
  • Higher pick rates vs. static shelving

Best for: High-velocity SKUs, case picks, replenishment from bulk

Multi-Level Pick Modules

Elevated pick levels accessed by stairs or lifts, multiplying pick faces in your footprint.

  • 2-3 levels of pick faces in one footprint
  • Conveyor integration between levels
  • Carton flow and shelving on upper levels
  • Maximizes expensive warehouse space

Best for: High-volume fulfillment, space-constrained facilities

Pallet Reserve Storage

Selective or high-density pallet rack storing bulk inventory that replenishes pick locations.

  • Selective rack for diverse SKU reserve
  • Push-back or drive-in for high-volume items
  • Positioned for efficient replenishment paths
  • Vertical storage maximizes cube utilization

Best for: Bulk inventory, seasonal stock, wholesale quantities

Layouts Designed for Pick Speed

In e-commerce, picking is your biggest labor cost. Storage layouts directly impact how many orders per hour your team can ship.

Velocity-Based Slotting

Fast movers in golden zone (waist height), close to pack stations. Slow movers up high or far from shipping.

Pick Path Optimization

Aisle layouts and zone configurations designed to minimize picker travel time between locations.

Batch & Wave Zones

Storage organized to support your WMS pick strategy—batch picking zones, wave staging areas, consolidation points.

Pack Station Proximity

High-velocity items positioned for short walks to pack and ship areas.

Built for Seasonal Scale

Holiday peaks aren't surprises—they're planned events. Your storage should be ready.

Expandable Configurations

Rack systems designed for easy expansion. Add bays, levels, or entirely new sections as volume grows.

Overflow Zones

Designated areas for peak season overflow—floor staging that converts to rack when needed.

Temporary Additions

Short-term rack rentals or quick-add sections for seasonal peaks without permanent capital investment.

Multi-Facility Planning

Consistent systems across multiple fulfillment centers for operational standardization.
