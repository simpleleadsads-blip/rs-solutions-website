Storage Zones in Manufacturing

Raw Material Storage

Incoming materials staged for production. Often requires heavy load capacity and may include cantilever systems for long stock like bar, tube, or sheet goods.

  • High load capacities (5,000+ lbs/level)
  • FIFO rotation for lot tracking
  • Located near receiving docks
  • Cantilever for long goods

WIP & Production Support

Work-in-progress staging between production steps. Positioned for efficient material flow to and from production lines.

  • Close to production floor
  • Accessible beam heights
  • Flow-through configurations
  • Queue management support

Finished Goods

Completed products ready for shipping. May require high-density storage for large volumes or selective access for diverse product lines.

  • Near shipping docks
  • Order staging areas
  • Flexible configurations
  • Seasonal capacity options

Racking Configurations for Manufacturing

Selective Pallet Rack

Direct access to every pallet. Essential for diverse part numbers, lot tracking, and FIFO rotation requirements common in manufacturing.

Common applications: Component storage, finished goods with diverse SKUs, quality hold areas

Drive-In Rack

High-density storage for homogeneous inventory. Ideal for bulk raw materials or high-volume finished goods with few SKUs.

Common applications: Bulk raw material storage, high-volume production runs, seasonal finished goods

Cantilever Rack

Open-front design for long, bulky, or awkward items that don't fit standard pallet configurations.

Common applications: Bar stock, tubing, pipes, sheet metal, lumber, extrusions, furniture components

Push-Back Rack

High-density with aisle-side access. Products push back on inclined carts, maintaining density without sacrificing pick speed.

Common applications: Raw materials with moderate SKU count, buffer stock, production staging

Manufacturing Storage Questions

Pallet rack can be engineered for virtually any load requirement. Standard heavy-duty systems handle 3,000-5,000 lbs per beam level. For heavier applications, structural systems can be designed for 8,000, 10,000, or even 15,000+ lbs per level. The limit is typically floor capacity rather than rack capacity.

Cantilever racks use horizontal arms extending from vertical columns, providing unobstructed front access for long items. Arms can be straight (for bundled stock) or inclined (for roll prevention). We size arm length, arm capacity, and column spacing based on your specific materials—whether that's 20-foot bar stock or 4x8 sheet goods.
