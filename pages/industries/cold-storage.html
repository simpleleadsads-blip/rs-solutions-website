Cold Storage Challenges

Corrosion Risk

Temperature cycling creates condensation. Standard painted steel corrodes quickly in cold environments.

Space Economics

Refrigerated space costs significantly more per square foot to build and operate. Every position must earn its keep.

Worker Exposure Limits

Workers can only spend limited time in freezer environments. Storage design must maximize efficiency.

Product Dating

Food products require strict FIFO rotation. Inventory management is non-negotiable for food safety.

Limited Floor Load

Insulated floors may have lower load capacity than ambient warehouses. Rack design must account for this.

Installation Constraints

Installing or modifying rack in operating cold facilities requires careful scheduling and cold weather protocols.

Built to Resist Corrosion

Standard rack paint fails in cold environments. Condensation during temperature cycles and door openings attacks unprotected steel. We specify corrosion-resistant finishes engineered for your specific conditions.

Hot-Dip Galvanizing

Components dipped in molten zinc for complete coverage. The gold standard for freezer environments. Provides sacrificial protection—zinc corrodes before steel.

Galvanized + Epoxy

Galvanized base with epoxy topcoat for maximum protection. Often used in the most demanding environments or where aesthetics matter.

Cold-Rolled Galvanized

Pre-galvanized steel formed into rack components. Good corrosion resistance at lower cost than hot-dip. Suitable for cooler (not freezer) applications.

Stainless Steel Hardware

Anchor bolts, safety clips, and fasteners in stainless steel to prevent hardware corrosion points.

High-Density Configurations for Cold Storage

When refrigeration costs $15-25+ per square foot annually, storage density directly impacts profitability. These configurations maximize pallets per cubic foot.

Drive-In Rack

Forklifts enter the rack structure to store pallets multiple positions deep. Maximum density for operations with few SKUs and LIFO inventory rotation acceptable.

  • Up to 75% more storage vs. selective
  • Ideal for blast freezer staging
  • Works well for single-commodity storage
  • LIFO inventory rotation

Best for: Bulk frozen storage, limited SKU operations, staging areas

Push-Back Rack

Pallets push back on inclined carts. High density with aisle-side access—no forklift entry into the rack structure.

  • 2-6 pallets deep per lane
  • Faster put/pull than drive-in
  • LIFO rotation with good density
  • Reduced forklift damage risk

Best for: Moderate SKU count, faster throughput needs, mixed inventory

Pallet Flow Rack

Gravity roller conveyors move pallets from load end to pick end. Automatic FIFO rotation—essential for date-coded products.

  • True FIFO rotation built in
  • Up to 20 pallets deep
  • Separate load and pick faces
  • Eliminates forklift travel in rack

Best for: Date-sensitive frozen foods, high-volume consistent SKUs

Double-Deep Selective

Two pallets deep accessed by reach truck with double-deep forks. Balance of density and selectivity.

  • About 40% more density than single-deep
  • Maintains reasonable selectivity
  • Works with diverse SKU profiles
  • Requires reach truck with extended forks

Best for: Mixed inventory, moderate density needs, operations with reach trucks

Storage by Temperature Zone

Cooler

35-40°F

Fresh produce, dairy, deli items. Pre-galvanized or epoxy-coated rack typically sufficient.

Freezer

0 to -10°F

Standard frozen foods. Hot-dip galvanized rack recommended for long-term durability.

Blast Freezer

-20°F & Below

Quick-freeze applications, ice cream. Hot-dip galvanized essential, steel becomes brittle at extreme cold.

Cold Environment Installation

Installing rack in cold facilities requires special planning and techniques that ambient warehouse projects don't.

Temperature Acclimation

Components should acclimate to ambient temperature before installation to prevent condensation issues during assembly.

Anchoring in Insulated Floors

Cold storage floors have insulation below the slab. Anchor specifications must account for this—typically longer anchors with reduced capacities.

Worker Rotation

Installation crews must rotate out of freezer environments regularly. This affects scheduling and crew sizing.

Operational Scheduling

Installation often happens during off-hours or with partial facility shutdowns to maintain temperature and avoid interference with operations.
