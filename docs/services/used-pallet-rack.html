Our Inspection Process

Not all used rack is equal. Here's how we ensure quality.

1

Visual Inspection

We examine every component for visible damage, rust, bends, and weld integrity.

2

Straightness Check

Uprights and beams are checked for bowing or twisting that affects structural integrity.

3

Connector Check

Beam connectors, safety pins, and anchor holes are inspected for damage and proper function.

4

Grading

Components are graded honestly so you know exactly what you're getting.

Our Grading System

Grade A - Excellent

Minor cosmetic wear only. Structurally perfect. Often from newer installations or low-use environments.

Grade B - Good

Normal wear including paint chips, surface rust, and minor scratches. Fully functional with no structural concerns.

Grade C - Fair

Heavier cosmetic wear but still structurally sound. Best value for non-visible applications.

What we don't sell: We reject any rack with structural damage, significant bowing, failed welds, or damage that affects load capacity. If it's not safe, we don't sell it.

When to Choose Used vs. New

Used Rack Is Great For:

  • Budget-conscious projects
  • Short-term or temporary storage
  • Expansion matching existing used rack
  • Non-customer-facing warehouses
  • Start-ups and growing operations
  • Quick-turnaround projects

Consider New Rack When:

  • Specific colors or finishes required
  • Unusual sizes needed
  • Matching existing new rack
  • Customer-facing environments
  • Very high seismic requirements
  • Full manufacturer warranty needed
